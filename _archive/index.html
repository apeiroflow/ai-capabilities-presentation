Point of View

How I Think About AI Monetisation

01

The Cost Reality

AI features carry real, variable costs: inference, quality loops, infrastructure. Unlike traditional SaaS, cost-to-serve shifts with usage patterns, model improvements, and scale. Any pricing model that ignores this breaks at volume.

02

The Value Disconnect

Users don't value AI by what it costs to run. They value it by what it helps them achieve. Pricing anchored to inference costs will always feel wrong. Price against outcomes: the workflow saved, the decision improved, the result delivered.

03

The Structure Problem

Credit, usage, tier, subscription: each model carries different trade-offs for adoption, revenue predictability, and competitive positioning. The right structure at launch is often wrong at scale. The framework has to be designed to evolve.

04

The Core Tension

Generous access drives adoption. Sustainable economics drives the business. Every AI pricing decision is a bet on where that line sits today, and how it moves as the category matures. Getting this right is the whole job.

The key insight: value is captured from user outcomes and workflows, not from underlying model costs. Design the framework around what users achieve, not what inference costs.

01 · The Thesis

Trading platform built for a world where AI agents execute on behalf of users. Blockchain infrastructure makes this viable: on-chain data, verifiable reasoning, and execution costs at 10% of the cheapest retail product.

Which AI capabilities to build, and at what cost? Mapped the vectors: chatbot, compliance engine, guidance, and advisory. Each carries a different cost structure, monetisation logic, and regulatory implication (robo-adviser licence, FSC).

03 · The Framework

Evaluated every AI vector against cost-to-serve, value capture (CAC, marketing, direct monetisation), and regulatory requirements. Designed a phased approach: build all functionality, then assess what the market values and what regulation permits.

04 · The Architecture

Tokenised assets expand the investable universe. On-chain data is transparent and agent-readable by design. Lower execution costs enable usage volumes that traditional infrastructure cannot serve.

Case Study 2

Minerals Marketplace

AI-Enabled Trading Platform · Pricing a New Category

01 · The Pricing Question

How do you price a platform where agents may do the work, not humans? Per seat, per agent, per volume, per outcome? Defined the commercial model for a financial marketplace where AI adoption is non-linear and future usage patterns are genuinely uncertain.

02 · The Cost Structure

Mapped the full cost base: data sourcing, hosting, security, audits, and token usage. Token usage is significant: multi-source intelligence workflows and continuous backtesting run on every signal. Variable, hard to predict, and growing.

03 · The Value Equation

For financial market participants, 5 basis points of outperformance on $100M to $1B AUM is worth millions. The platform needed to price as a rounding error to the client while remaining material to the business.

04 · The Commercial Architecture

Evaluated per-seat, per-agent, and volume-based structures against projected AI adoption curves. Designed data access and surfacing patterns to enable agent-driven workflows: a commercial architecture built for an agent-first future.
